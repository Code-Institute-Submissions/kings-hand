About King's Hand

A pile of stones ceases to be a pile of stones the moment a man begins to think of it carrying within himself a picture of the cathedral, said the famous Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. Nikola Lozić, a versatile young artist from Cork whose love for art started at an early age while trying to copy his uncle's drawings, thinks the same way. As a child, he always made family members sit still as he tried to draw them.

Being an artist is not a matter of personal choice, nor a matter of schooling, although there is something small in that as well. First of all, one does not become an artist. You are simply an artist or you are not. Professionals perceive art as an occupation, although art is much more than that, those who live for art will tell you, but not necessarily from it. Art is a vocation of life and that constant and constant creative inner scream that you have to somehow let out, one way or another.

“The first drawing I remember was a picture of my parents when I was about 7-8 years old. I gave it to my mother for her birthday and that drawing was great for me then, but I don't think I would be thrilled if I looked at it now ", Nikola joked and added:" I never thought I would do drawing. I always thought it would be music or some activities related to my bachelor’s degree in physiotherapy. At that time, like most people, I did not think that one could make a living from art ", Nikola explained to us. To make the picture, more precisely the drawing, look as realistic as possible, he points out, the most important thing is patience, a lot of practice and a good eye for details, a play of shadows and lights. If the drawing has no depth and good shadows the drawing will look blank and will not leave a good impression, it will look unfinished. The same goes for tattoos because if the image does not blend in with the skin tone, it will lose its strength over time, and therefore the aesthetics of the tattoo will not be the same.

Cover-up

You have a tattoo you have grown out of, or from a time in your life you would rather forget?

At King's Hand we specialize in creative reinvention of unwanted tattoos, as they are smaller than covering it up with bigger and darker design. Another thing to consider when deciding between removal or cover-up is that covering up an unwanted tattoo is generally shorter and much cheaper than tattoo removal.

Poorly applied, faded, or light tattoos are the easiest to cover and can only be covered up using the same or darker ink. White ink may sometimes be applied to lighten the old tattoo before proceeding with the cover-up. For more information on cover ups and touch ups you can message us directly, please don’t forget to add an image of the tattoo you are planning to cover/ rework.

Laser Removal

Are you unhappy with a tattoo and want it removed?

Laser tattoo removal is effective treatment for the safe removal of tattoos with minimal pain and no lasting harm to the skin.

Laser light of a particular frequency is passed through the skin, where it can separate the tattoo ink into more modest particles. The immune system can easily eliminate these from the body. Initial consultation lasts 20 minutes and a normal treatment will take around 15 to 20 minutes. It usually takes 3 sessions to completely remove the tattoo
